This post summarizes a lecture survey on **Retrieval-Augmented 3D Generation** presented at KAIST CS588. The survey explores how external 3D contexts can assist neural networks in generating 3D content more efficiently.

## Survey Overview

The core objective of this survey is to address how to prevent hallucinations and incorporate up-to-date information without constantly updating model parameters. By leveraging retrieval, models can overcome the limitations of 3D datasets compared to 2D vision or NLP models.

### Why 3D Generation Needs Retrieval
* **Memory**: Externalizing information reduces the burden on model parameters.
* **Generalization**: Accessing various views in a database improves model robustness.
* **Dynamics**: Retrieval allows for the integration of new information during the generation process.
* **Details**: High-frequency details can be retrieved to refine the final output.

## 3D Representation and Embedding

### Diverse 3D Representations
The survey covers multiple ways to represent 3D data, including Voxels, Meshes, Point Clouds, and Implicit Functions.
* **Point Cloud**: A set of coordinates $(N, 3)$ that is memory-efficient and simple, though it lacks topological information.


### Multi-modal Alignment (Connecting 3D to CLIP)
A key part of the survey is how 3D data is aligned with the CLIP space to enable cross-modal retrieval.
* **ULIP / ULIP-2**: Uses (image, text, point cloud) triplets to pre-align 3D encoders.
* **Uni3D**: A scalable framework that uses Vision Transformers (ViT) as a unified 3D encoder, scaling up to 1 billion parameters.

## Frameworks for Retrieval-Augmented Generation

### Capturing Similarity
The survey highlights that semantic similarity alone is insufficient for high-quality 3D generation.
* **Fine-grained Similarity**: Utilizing part-level retrieval (e.g., specific armrests or wheels for a chair).
* **Style Similarity**: Focusing on materials, textures, and the overall design of the object.

### Diffusion Models in 3D
The survey categorizes 3D diffusion into two main paradigms:
1.  **Direct 3D Diffusion**: Generating 3D representations like point clouds (Point-E) or implicit functions (Shap-E).
2.  **Multi-view 2D Diffusion**: Generating 2D images from different angles and combining them into a 3D object.

## Challenges and Solutions

| Challenge                        | Surveyed Solutions                                                                                       |
| :------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Hallucination in Novel Views** | **Zero-1-to-3**: Conditioning the model with reference images and camera poses.                          |
| **Geometry Consistency**         | **Phidias**: Using Canonical Coordinate Maps (CCM) from retrieved references to guide geometry learning. |

## Conclusion
This survey concludes that the main challenge in 3D generation lies in improving quality through spatio-temporal consistency and efficient data representations. Retrieving "useful" 3D objects as references is more important than simple matching to ensure the generated model is practical and detailed.
